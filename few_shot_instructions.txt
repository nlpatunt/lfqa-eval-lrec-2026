### Instruction:
You must answer only "yes" or "no". Do not add any explanation or rationale behind it.
Decide whether the user’s question qualifies as a Long-Form Question Answering (LFQA) (suitable for a long-form explanatory answer) according to the definition and annotation flow below.
Important:
Strict response format: yes or no
Use your own intuition instead of simple keyword-matching

Definition:
A long-form question must express a single, well-defined information need that requires a detailed, multi-sentence answer involving exposition, explanation, reasoning, exploration, or description of the process. These questions are usually complex or open-ended, often asking “why” or “how” about a process, reason, or process. It must not combine multiple distinct or loosely related sub-questions, request personal advice, express individual preferences, or seek personal opinions. They demand thorough, paragraph-length responses. It cannot be answered sufficiently with a single fact, formula application, calculation, only code, or by generating creative content (poem, joke, story, music, song lyrics, audio, picture).

Annotation flow:
Step 1: Simple or invalid question check
Is the question asking for a single fact, a yes/no response, only code generation, direct formula calculation, or creative content (e.g., poem, joke, story, music, song lyrics, image, or audio)?
OR, does the question combine multiple distinct or loosely related sub-questions?
OR, does the question request personal advice, express individual preferences, or seek personal opinions?

If yes to any of the above, then classify it as Not LFQA and reply no.
If no, move to Step 2.

Step 2:  Exposition, explanation, reasoning, exploration, or description of the process Development Check
Does the question express a single, well-defined information need?
AND does answering require a multi-sentence answer involving exposition, reasoning, explanation, exploration, or description of a process or concept?

If yes to both, then classify it as an LFQA and reply yes
If no, then it is not LFQA, and reply no
